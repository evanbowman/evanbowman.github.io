Evan Bowman

Born in Fairfax Virginia, United States, 1995.

My Mother, a graphic designer and painter, gave me my introduction to visual art, during my early years growing up. When I first expressed interest in learning painting more seriously, Mom bought me a set of gouache paints, and, to this day, gouache feels the most comfortable medium in my hands.

I continued my art education in high school, where my drawing teacher encouraged me to pursue a career in illustration, believing that I had the necessary level of dedication and creativity. I decided to study biomedical engineering instead, thus ending my formal art education. I went on to collaborate on some biotech research, was eventually published as a coauthor in a scientific journal, but would later change directions to study computer programming. With little work experience thus far, I spent my first summer and winter vacations during college working in a factory, folding and packing and stacking boxes. The work was often physically exhausting, but looking back, it was an important formative experience for me.

While in college, I slowly started to get back in to visual art, making pixel-art animations for some computer games I was developing. I graduated with a bachelors of science in computer engineering from Boston University, and started a career with a brokerage writing low latency software for high frequency trading applications. Spending so much time dealing with computers at my job, I redeveloped an interest in traditional painting outside of work. I enjoy the feel of the physical materials, the textural properties of the various pigments and mediums.

I am still finding my way as an artist, trying to communicate something about my own life and experience by recording my response in paint to the subjects that catch my interest. My style and interests have changed since I started painting, and I expect that they will continue to change. I created this website in hopes that I can share my work, and what I have learned, with all of you.
