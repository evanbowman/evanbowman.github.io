Evan Bowman
Portfolio Notes Bio

Evan Bowman was born in Fairfax, Virginia in 1995, and grew up in Albany New York. Although he works professionally as an engineer, writing low-level systems software, Evan has a lifelong interest in color and design. His earliest artistic influence came from his mother, a graphic designer and painter. He continued his art education in high school, where his drawing teacher encouraged him to pursue a career in illustration, but Evan decided to study engineering instead, thus ending his formal art education. He has a degree in computer engineering from Boston University, and has lived in the Boston area since late 2013.

Once a practical engineer, largely uninterested in abstract mathematical theory, Evan ultimately became bored with certain aspects of software development, finding them insufficently challenging, and now spends much of his time outside of work playing with recursive algorithms in LISP interpreters, and programming Gameboy consoles in C++, and in ARM and Z80 assembly languages.

In his choice of artistic subjects, Evan finds inspiration in the landscape of the Northeastern United States, where he grew up. He especially admires the work of printmaker Felix Vallotton, as well as hundreds of other artists, particulary from the baroque era, Les Nabis, realism, and nineteenth century art more generally.

Evan works obsessively on his paintings, but has never exhibited or sold anything or had much recognition. He became increasingly reclusive during the 2020 pandemic, and lives under a growing, cluttered pile of his prints and sketches. He maintains a painting studio near Boston, where he has crammed some cabinets, easels, and a large etching press, into a spare bedroom of his apartment.
